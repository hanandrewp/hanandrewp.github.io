Deadly car crashes still affect some states more than others

Since 1990, the national rate of death due to car accidents has been halved, from 2.1 deaths per 100 million miles driven, to 1.1 deaths. Still, some states perform better than others when it comes to vehicular safety. Massachusetts has the lowest fatality rate, with only 0.6 deaths per 100 million miles driven. Montana lags behind the rest of the nation, with 2.0 deaths.
